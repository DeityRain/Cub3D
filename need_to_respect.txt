=========================================

The first raycasting step is:

Cast one ray for each vertical column of the screen

so for x = 0 to WIN_WIDTH - 1
Convert the screen column into a camera coordinate

cameraX = 2 * x / WIN_WIDTH - 1
this gives a value from -1 to 1
Build the ray direction

rayDirX = dir_x + plane_x * cameraX
rayDirY = dir_y + plane_y * cameraX
So the very first raycasting goal is just:

take the player direction
take the camera plane
compute a ray direction for each column
That is the base of the whole system.

After that, the next steps are:

find which grid square the ray enters first
use DDA to march through the map
detect the wall hit
If you want, I can guide you through just step 1 next: setting up cameraX and the ray direction.

=========================================

- Next logical step is to use the DDA hit result to draw the scene:

    Compute the perpendicular wall distance
    Convert that distance into wall slice height
    Determine the exact wall hit position
    Choose the texture side
    Draw the vertical wall stripe column by column

- After that, the usual order is:

    floor and ceiling rendering
    player movement / rotation
    z-buffer if you have sprites
    
    So in practice: DDA gives the hit; the next step is wall projection and texture mapping.
